## Key Findings

### FactCC (Salesforce, EMNLP 2020)
- **Approach**: Weakly-supervised model for verifying factual consistency
- **Training data**: Generated by applying rule-based transformations (entity swaps, negation, etc.) to source sentences
- **Joint training on three tasks**:
  1. Identify whether sentences remain factually consistent after transformation
  2. Extract supporting span in source documents
  3. Extract inconsistent span in summary sentence
- **Key advantage**: Provides span-level evidence for consistency/inconsistency judgments
- **Available**: github.com/salesforce/factCC

### DAE - Dependency Arc Entailment (Goyal & Durrett, 2020)
- **Approach**: Decomposes factuality assessment to dependency arc level
- **Process**: For each dependency arc in summary, predict whether the relationship is entailed by the source
- **Architecture**: Head and tail embeddings of arc concatenated with dependency label encoding, fed to classifier
- **Key advantages**:
  1. Finer granularity than sentence-level methods
  2. Can localize errors in the output (which specific relations are unsupported)
  3. Outperforms sentence-level methods and QG-based approaches
- **Available**: github.com/tagoyal/dae-factuality

### Comparison
- DAE's arc-level decomposition finds more fine-grained errors than FactCC's sentence-level approach
- FactCC's span extraction provides human-interpretable evidence
- Both outperform simple NLI approaches but are surpassed by newer QA-based and LLM-based methods

### Relevance to Consolidation
- FactCC's span-level evidence is valuable for debugging consolidation errors
- DAE's error localization helps identify which specific facts were distorted
- Both methods verify faithfulness (no hallucination) but not completeness (no omission)
- For consolidation, would need to be combined with coverage metrics to ensure both directions are checked
