# Progressive Summarization (Building a Second Brain)

## Source
Tiago Forte. "Building a Second Brain: The Definitive Introductory Guide." Forte Labs.

## Four-Layer Methodology

1. **Layer 1 (Raw Excerpts)**: Original captured chunks of text
2. **Layer 2 (Bold)**: Bold the main points — keywords, key phrases, resonant sentences
3. **Layer 3 (Highlighting)**: Highlight within the bolded text for especially long/valuable notes
4. **Layer 4 (Executive Summary)**: Bullet point summary in your own words (reserved for the most valuable sources)

## Key Design Properties

- **Each layer preserves access to all lower layers**: Non-destructive; you can always drill down
- **10-20% compression per layer**: Each layer includes no more than 10-20% of the previous
- **Progressive, not all-at-once**: Summarization happens incrementally over time as you revisit notes
- **Part of CODE**: Capture → Organize → Distill → Express

## Relevance to Incremental Consolidation

Progressive Summarization is inherently incremental:
- Notes are captured first, distilled later
- Distillation happens at the point of use, not at capture time
- Multiple passes refine understanding without losing source material
- The layered structure means consolidation can be partial and progressive

## Key Insight for /consolidate

The "distill at point of use, not at capture time" principle suggests that incremental consolidation doesn't need to fully process each new source immediately. A lighter initial integration (claim extraction + matching) followed by deeper consolidation on demand may be more practical than eager full processing.
