### D1 (UX moat — presence, source toggle, permissions, embeds)

#### 1. **Cmd+Z the Agent**
*The cleanest six-second clip we have.* Press ⌘Z once and the agent's last paragraph rolls back, line by line. Press ⌘Z again and yours stays untouched. Press ⌘⇧Z and the agent's text comes back. The keyboard shortcut every dev has muscle memory for, doing the thing every dev has wanted to do at least once. The single most-converged-upon micro-moment in the entire 20-agent corpus (six independent agents). Quote-tweet bait. The Bolt-equivalent.
**Hits:** D1, D5
**Inspiration:** Bolt.new's single-action demo; per-author undo as a category invention.
**What you see:** "⌘Z just undid the AI. Not your last keystroke. The AI's last paragraph."

### My read on which spine has the deepest convergence density

**Spine 2 (Power-Tool-Pride) carries the highest convergence weight.** Cmd+Z the agent appeared in six independent agents; two-cursor co-editing in seven; demo-agent-on-init in seven; share-artifact-as-primitive in eight; benchmark-as-flywheel in five; manifesto pattern in five+. **Spine 1 (Warm-Ceremony) is the second-strongest by convergence**, especially because the deterministic-mascot cluster (10 agents) is the *largest* convergence tier. But it lives in a different tonal universe and has higher dev-audience-skepticism risk.

**Spine 3 (Wonder-and-Magic) has the highest cross-tribe ceiling** but the lowest convergence density per surface. It's the spine most likely to produce a permanent dent in brand identity if executed perfectly, and the spine most likely to flop if execution is mediocre.

Best move (out of scope for me to recommend, but visible from the data): **threads can mix.** Cmd+Z the agent (Spine 2) and Bramble (Spine 1) and the Karpathy Welcome (Spines 2+3) are not in conflict. The corpus suggests a launch can carry **Spine 2 as the primary register, with Spine 1's hatching ceremony as the warm onboarding moment, and Spine 3's openknowledge.dev/live as the always-on attractor.** That would honor the convergence on all three of the highest-density clusters simultaneously.
