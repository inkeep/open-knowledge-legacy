# Positioning & Strategic Direction Evidence

## Current Positioning

### Tagline: "Your team's knowledge base"
### GitHub description: "The fastest knowledge base for growing teams"

### Key Marketing Claims:
- "Blazing fast editor" - speed is the primary differentiator
- "Millisecond response times"
- Real-time collaborative editing
- "Built in public" with public changelog
- "Open source" (technically source-available under BSL)
- Self-hosted option alongside cloud

### Positioning Against:
- **vs Notion**: Simpler, faster, focused on docs (not databases/project management). Outline doesn't try to be an all-in-one workspace.
- **vs Confluence**: Modern, fast, real-time collaboration (vs Confluence's save-then-publish model). Positioned as the alternative for teams leaving Atlassian's self-hosted products.
- **vs Google Docs**: Structured knowledge base with hierarchy, permissions, search. Not just documents but organized knowledge.

## Strategic Direction Signals

### Product Trajectory (from changelog analysis):

**2024 Focus**: Editor maturity
- Table improvements (merging, sorting, backgrounds)
- Image viewer rebuild
- v1.0.0 milestone (Oct 2024)

**2025 Focus**: Collaboration & integrations
- GitHub/Linear/GitLab rich embeds
- Public collection sharing
- Group mentions, link mentions
- PDF embeds, Draw.io diagrams

**2026 Focus (so far)**: AI & agent integration
- MCP server (Feb 2026) - major signal
- Toggle blocks, table improvements
- Passkey authentication
- GitLab integration

### AI Direction:
- AI Answers shipped earlier (pre-2025)
- MCP shipped Feb 2026 - this is the most significant AI/agent move
- AI is additive (search enhancement) not transformative (not reimagining workflows)
- No AI writing, generation, or organization features

### Hiring Signals:
- No public job listings found
- Tom Moor appears to be primary developer (also works at Linear as Head of Eng)
- Bootstrapped = conservative growth
- No signals of building an AI team

### Growth Tailwinds:
- Atlassian shutting down self-hosted products drove demand
- Growing interest in self-hosted alternatives
- 37.9k GitHub stars indicates strong developer interest
- BSL allows self-hosting which appeals to privacy-conscious orgs

## Competitive Vulnerabilities

1. **Small team**: Primarily one person (Tom Moor) who also works at Linear. Limited development velocity compared to well-funded competitors.
2. **BSL licensing**: Prevents community forks and competing services. The "open source" positioning is arguably misleading.
3. **No plugin ecosystem**: No extensibility model means all features must come from core team.
4. **Editor gap vs Notion**: Missing databases, kanban, timeline views, and advanced block types.
5. **AI story is thin**: AI Answers is table-stakes RAG. MCP is read/write access but not agent-native.
6. **No markdown fidelity**: Internal ProseMirror JSON creates portability concerns for developer-oriented users.

## Company Financial Profile

- Bootstrapped and profitable
- No external investors
- Revenue from cloud subscriptions + self-hosted enterprise licenses
- Sustainable but growth-constrained business model
- Offers complimentary accounts for climate change organizations + nonprofit discounts
