## Pricing Tiers (2026)

| Plan | Price (annual) | Price (monthly) | AI Included | Key Limits |
|------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|------------|
| Free | $0 | $0 | No | 5MB file upload, 10 guests |
| Plus | $10/user/mo | $12/user/mo | No | 100 guests |
| Business | $20/user/mo | $24/user/mo | Yes (unlimited) | 250 guests |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Yes (unlimited) | Custom |

### AI Agent Credits
- Free trial period (through May 2026)
- Then $10 per 1,000 credits (Business/Enterprise only)

### AI Pricing History
- Pre-May 2025: $10/user/month add-on on any plan
- May 2025+: Bundled into Business/Enterprise only
- Strategic move: forces teams wanting AI to upgrade to $20/user/mo minimum

## Competitive Positioning
- Self-described "connected workspace" -> "AI workspace" (2025+)
- Competing with: Airtable, Monday.com, Confluence, Google Workspace
- Product surface: Docs + Databases + Projects + Mail + Calendar + Sites + AI Agents
- Growing enterprise focus with SSO, SAML, audit logs, row-level permissions

## Implications for Agent-Native Platforms

Notion's pricing strategy of bundling AI into $20/user/mo Business tier means teams pay for Notion's LLM compute whether they want it or not. An agent-native platform with zero LLM compute in the product could offer a structurally lower price point while letting users bring their own AI. The $10/1000 credits for agents also creates ongoing compute costs that compound -- a platform where agents use their own compute avoids this entirely.
